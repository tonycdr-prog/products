What flat can you actually afford… once service charge is included?

Most mortgage calculators ignore the leasehold reality: service charge, ground rent, and insurance can add hundreds per month. This tool gives you a conservative monthly budget and a maximum purchase price you can sanity-check before you fall in love with a listing.

How it works (conservative on purpose)

  1. Estimate a safe housing budget (default 33% of net income minus debts).
  2. Subtract leasehold costs (service charge + ground rent + buildings insurance).
  3. Whatever is left is your mortgage payment budget → we convert that into a max mortgage + max price.

Not financial advice. Use this as a screening tool before you speak to a broker/lender.

FAQ

Why use net income instead of gross?

Because your month-to-month cashflow is what gets squeezed by service charge increases. Lenders use gross + stress tests; this tool is for your sanity check.

Does this account for council tax / utilities?

Not yet. We keep it focused on leasehold running costs. In Plus, we’ll add optional council tax + utilities + travel to build a fuller budget.

Is this financial advice?

No. It’s a calculator + rules-of-thumb tool. Always confirm with a broker/lender and read the lease/service charge accounts.
